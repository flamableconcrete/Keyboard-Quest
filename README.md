# ⌨️ Keyboard Quest

> **Type words. Smash goblins. Save the world.**

## What Is This?

The evil **Typemancer** has stolen letters from the alphabet and scattered them across the land. Villages are being raided by goblins. Ogres are hoarding vowels. Skeletons are swarm-dancing for no good reason.

Only one hero can stop them: **you** — armed with nothing but a keyboard.

## 🎮 Play It Now

**[keyboardquest.giantsoup.com](https://keyboardquest.giantsoup.com)**

No download. No install. Just open it and start typing.

## How It Works

1. Words appear on screen
2. You type them
3. Monsters explode (or run away, or collapse into a pile of bones)
4. You get cooler and more powerful

That's it. That's the game. But also there are **5 worlds**, **boss battles**, **companions**, **spells**, and a final showdown with the Typemancer himself. So also it's kind of a lot.

## 🔤 You Start With 7 Letters

Don't panic! You begin with just the home row keys (`a s d f j k l`). As you defeat mini-bosses, they drop stolen letters back into the alphabet. Beat **Knuckle the Ogre, Keeper of E** and suddenly `E` is yours. Forever.

Every letter is guarded by a boss with an extremely dramatic name.

## 🏆 Boss Hall of Shame

Some of the villains standing between you and victory:

- **Grizzlefang** — armor made of broken keyboard keys *(World 1 Final Boss)*
- **Nessa the Snake Dancer, Keeper of N** — she coils. she strikes. she hisses at your typos.
- **Rend the Red, Keeper of R** — strikes from the shadows like a dramatic red shadow
- **Quixel the Quillmaster, Keeper of Q** — scratches rare words into the walls
- **Xeron the Hexed Exile, Keeper of X** — casts words as weapons (very meta)
- **Zindark the Zealot, Keeper of Z** — honestly Z is rare, this feels personal
- **The Typemancer** — floats above his throne, weaving letters into destruction, 5 phases, absolutely unhinged

## ⭐ Scoring

Type accurately, type fast, earn stars. More stars = more XP = leveling up = becoming unstoppable. A goblin cook once challenged someone to an **exploding cheese contest** and that person got 5 stars. You can too.

## 🧒 Who Is This For?

Kids learning to type! The game starts super easy (7 keys!) and slowly introduces new letters as you get comfortable. By the time you reach World 5, you'll be using the whole keyboard — and you won't even notice because you'll be too busy fighting a jester.

Parents: this is basically typing practice disguised as a quest. Teachers: same.
